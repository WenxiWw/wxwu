User-guided Constrianed Motion Planning with Reactive Obstacle Avoidance

This is an approach to generating real-time motion plans for point-to-point tasks using a single successful human demonstration. The motion generation is based on screw linear interpolation, which allows us to respect the underlying geometric constraints that characterize the task and are implicitly present in the demonstration. An original reactive collision avoidance algoriothm is integrated in the planner. The Rapidly Expanding Plane-oriented Escaping Trees (REPET) algorithm enables the robot to generate the path to avoid obstacles in real time. 

S*: A Solution to the Safety-Performance Tradeoff in Robot Motion Planning

The safety-aware time-optimal planner relies on a modified version of A* algorithm embedding the concepts of safe motion unit from our previous research. This work, instead, proposes solving the time-optimal approach explicitly embedding safety-constraints in a time-optimal-path-graph-search approach which combines both safety-constraints in terms of maximum-velocity (a function of the reflected mass) and joint-limit capabilities. Our planner therefore produces an efficient time-optimal manipulation plan by choosing trajectories that lead to reduced reflected mass -- and therefore higher safety velocities The proposed approach simplifies the safety time-optimal trade-off been suitable for online implementation.  

Stabilization of the Humanoid Robot NAO

This project adds a stabilization by stepping functionality to the NAO RoboCup HULKs framework. The NAO robot is therefore modeled as linearized inverted pendulum. The implemented stabilization strategy is based on the capture point theory and added to the walking generator of the HULKs framework. In experiments in simulation (Simrobot) and on the real NAO robot the capture point stepping is verified. The results show a higher stability when being pushed for the enabled stepping stabilization compared to the disabled case. 

Gesture-controlled Car with Obstacle Avoidance

The motivation for this project was to design a vehicle that can be used by disabled people. A vehicle that can be controlled by different hand gestures of the human is designed in this project. The hand tilt is captured by the IMU sensor on the glove. Then, the command is sent through telecommulication module to the controller, thus controlling the moving velocity and direction of the robot car. The Infrared sensors enable the car to stop automatically when there are obstacles in the way.
